Hey there, app enthusiasts! Today, we're diving into the tale of NGL, the app that took "surprise billing" to a whole new level. Imagine signing up for a one-time fee and getting hit with a weekly $9.99 charge instead. Yikes! Let's break down what happened and how you can claim your part of the $4.5 million settlement.

### The Not-So-Anonymous Charges

So, what's the deal with NGL? This anonymous messaging app promised its Pro users a magical one-time fee experience. But plot twist! Users were instead treated to a recurring weekly fee—up to $9.99—without giving a thumbs-up. Talk about a surprise subscription!

The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) wasn't amused and took NGL to court, resulting in a $4.5 million settlement. Now, this cash is waiting to be claimed by you savvy consumers who got caught in NGL's pricing trap.

### How to Get Your Money Back

If you were a victim of this sneaky fee fiasco between January 2022 and July 2024, you might be eligible for a refund. Here's how to make sure you don't miss out:

1. **Check Your Calendar**: Circle April 6, 2026, on your calendar. That's your deadline to apply for a refund.
   
2. **Visit the FTC's NGL Settlement Page**: Head over to [ftc.gov/ngl](http://ftc.gov/ngl) to start your refund claim.

3. **Eligibility Check**: You need to be 18 or older to apply. If you're not, rope in your favorite adult to help out!

4. **Understand the Fine Print**: Applying for a refund doesn't mean you automatically get one. The FTC will review claims and calculate refunds based on the number of eligible applicants, among other factors. So, fingers crossed!

5. **Need Assistance?**: If you're feeling lost in the refund process, reach out to the FTC's claims administrator at 1-800-351-7161 or drop an email at info@NGLRefund.com.

### Takeaways

- **Stay Alert**: Always double-check subscription terms before hitting that "buy" button. Surprise fees are about as welcome as a skunk at a garden party.
  
- **Spread the Word**: Know someone who might be eligible? Share this info! After all, no one likes being left out of a settlement party.

In the end, remember that staying informed is your best defense against sneaky charges. Until next time, keep those eyes peeled and wallets safe!
